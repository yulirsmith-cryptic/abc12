About the CAB

Real decision-making
authority.

The PCHW CAB is a compensated, community-led advisory body. Members don't advise from the margins — they hold genuine authority over the deliverables that go directly to the California Department of Health Care Services (DHCS).

CAB members receive a $1,500 stipend, along with language access support, childcare assistance, and transportation reimbursement. Your participation costs you nothing.

Beyond the stipend, members build leadership and advocacy skills, shape maternal health policy in California, and join a network of advocates statewide.

We meet regularly over 12 months — primarily virtually, with in-person convenings during the project period.

Three workstreams
i. Define the Perinatal CHW Develop a community-informed definition of "Perinatal Community Health Worker" for adoption by DHCS, managed care plans, and training programs.
ii. Defend the Medi-Cal CHW benefit Serve as an organized community voice in state policy. Produce briefs, support public testimony, and protect the Medi-Cal CHW benefit for birthing families.
iii. Strengthen integrated care models Co-develop a replicable framework for licensed midwife–PCHW collaboration: shared care plans, communication protocols, and dyadic family-centered assessment tools.
Eligibility
i. Community Health Worker Current CHW, certified or working under a CHW title
ii. 1+ year of experience At least one year in community health work
iii. California resident Currently living and working in California
